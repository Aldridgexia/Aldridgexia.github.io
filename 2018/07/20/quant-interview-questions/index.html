1 Regression Model

1.1 Linear Regression

1.1.1 Assumption

  1. linear relationship: $Y = X \beta + \epsilon$
  2. zero mean of errors: $\mathbb{E} [\epsilon_i] = 0,\ \forall i=1,\dots,n$
  3. constant variance of errors: $\text{var}(\epsilon_i) = \sigma^2,\ \forall i=1,\dots,n$
  4. uncorrelated errors: $\mathbb{E} [\epsilon_i \epsilon_j] = 0,\ i \neq j$
  5. no perfect multicollinearity: $\rho(x_i,x_j) \neq \pm 1,\ i \neq j$, where $\rho(x_i,x_j)$ is the correlation of regressor $x_i$ and $x_j$
  6. $\epsilon$ and $x_i$ are independent
